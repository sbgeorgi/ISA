University of Arizona Agrivoltaics Timeline

2017

Early Research and Development

UArizona researchers initiated agrivoltaic experiments at the Biosphere 2 facility. Led by Associate Professor Greg Barron-Gafford, the team cultivated crops beneath elevated solar panels, revealing increased fruit production and reduced water usage.

2018

Recognition and Awards

Fast Company magazine honored the project as a finalist in its World Changing Ideas Awards, underscoring its potential to revolutionize sustainable agriculture and energy practices.

2020

Expansion and Community Engagement

The initiative expanded to local schools in the Tucson Unified School District, integrating agrivoltaic systems into campuses and engaging K-12 students in STEM education.

2023

Advancements and Funding

UArizona's research received a $1.2 million grant from the U.S. Department of Energy to explore integrating crop cultivation and livestock grazing within solar farms.

Read more
2023

Innovative Campus Implementations

The ENR2 building now features a rooftop agrivoltaic system combining solar panels with a green roof, studying the synergy between vegetation and PV panels.

Read more
2024

Global Recognition and Leadership

UArizona co-hosted the 5th World Conference on Agrivoltaics in June 2024 with NREL, gathering international experts to exchange innovative research and practices.
