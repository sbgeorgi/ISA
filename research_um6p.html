Integration of Agrivoltaics at UM6P

Mohammed VI Polytechnic University (UM6P) in Benguérir, Morocco, is a leading institution in agricultural research and innovation in the region. With a focus on sustainable agriculture, green energy, and technology transfer, UM6P's Agricultural Innovation and Technology Transfer Center (AITTC) is at the forefront of agrivoltaic development in Africa.

AITTC serves as an experimental farm and demonstration hub for new agri-tech solutions [58]. UM6P has integrated solar technology into crop production systems, establishing pilot agrivoltaic setups to study performance under Moroccan climate conditions. In 2022, UM6P launched its first agrivoltaic experimental unit, joining a global movement to evaluate agrivoltaics in diverse environments [59]. This collaborative project involves international experts and leverages Morocco's strong solar energy research sector.

Research and Experimental Facilities

UM6P’s AITTC provides state-of-the-art facilities to advance agrivoltaic research, including:

  • Soil, plant, and water laboratories for analyzing the effects of panel shading [60] [61].
  • Large plant nursery for testing crop varieties under varied light conditions.
  • Experimental farm for side-by-side comparisons of conventional and agrivoltaic farming.
  • Drip irrigation systems to precisely manage water and assess water-use efficiency.

Researchers from UM6P’s School of Agriculture (ESAFE) and the Green Energy Park collaborate on these trials, combining agronomic and solar energy expertise. UM6P's AgriEdge unit collaborates with French agrivoltaic companies and engages with Moroccan agribusiness stakeholders to test agrivoltaics in real-world farm settings [62] [63].

Key Focus Areas and Preliminary Findings

UM6P’s agrivoltaic research addresses practical questions for African agriculture, focusing on:

  • Identifying optimal crops and cultivars for agrivoltaic systems in hot, bright climates.
  • Evaluating the response of staple and high-value crops to shading in Moroccan conditions.
  • Exploring "climate-smart" and traditional crops for ecological benefits in agrivoltaic systems.
  • Quantifying water savings and improved growing conditions due to panel shade in Morocco’s context.
  • Optimizing solar panel arrangement and technology (height, tilt, spacing, transparency) for agriculture.
  • Monitoring solar panel performance and efficiency gains from crop transpiration cooling [64] [65].

Preliminary findings align with global trends, showing reduced plant heat stress and water demand. Initial data from UM6P indicates water savings and more stable growing conditions, crucial for climate change adaptation [66].

Role in Africa and Collaborations

UM6P aims to be a pan-African university, focusing on agrivoltaic solutions for African smallholder farmers and agribusiness. UM6P is a key partner in the EU-funded AgriEngage project, promoting agri-entrepreneurship in African higher education [67] [68]. Agrivoltaics is seen as a high-potential innovation for entrepreneurial ventures in Africa.

  • Collaborations with CGIAR and African research institutes, including IITA [69] [70].
  • Links to OCP Group for large-scale demonstration projects and CSR initiatives in Africa.
  • Alignment with Moroccan government strategies (Green Morocco Plan, Generation Green) [71] [72].
  • Contribution to national dialogues on agrivoltaics in Morocco [73].
  • International knowledge exchange and hosting delegations on agrivoltaic policy and economics.

Future Funding and Expansion

UM6P's agrivoltaic program has strong potential for growth through diverse funding avenues:

  • EU research grants (Horizon Europe, PRIMA) for collaborative projects.
  • Joint proposals with UNAM, UA, and other partners to compare agrivoltaics across continents.
  • Development bank and climate finance mechanisms (African Development Bank, World Bank).
  • Private sector sponsorship from Moroccan energy companies and international solar developers.
  • Spin-off company creation and commercialization through UM6P Ventures [74] [75].
  • NGO and philanthropic funding focused on African food security and climate resilience.

UM6P’s AITTC is poised to drive agrivoltaics adoption in Africa, contributing to climate-resilient and energy-integrated agriculture.
